# Phishing 
Phishing is a common type of cybercrime where attackers try to steal sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, and personal data. This is usually done through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear to be legitimate.

For example, a student may receive an email that looks like it is from a bank or a trusted website. The email may ask the user to click on a link to verify their account details. When the user clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake website that looks real. If they enter their login credentials or banking information, the attacker gains access to their account.

Phishing attacks often target students and people who are not fully aware of online scams. The consequences can include financial loss, identity theft, and misuse of personal data.

To avoid phishing attacks, users should always verify the source of emails and messages before clicking any links. They should check website URLs carefully and avoid sharing sensitive information online. Awareness and careful online behavior are key to preventing such cybercrimes.
